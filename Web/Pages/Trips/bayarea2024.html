Although this was not a train trip per se - I had merely taken the train once or twice during a much longer trip - I felt like it deserved to be mentioned as it was quite pleasant, and people may not expect so.

Travel path

  1. One short ride, getting on at Larkspur station and off on the very next stop - San Rafael - 7 minutes later

What I did in the Bay Area

Little of what I did relates to this train website, but I don't wish to be so brief, so here's some context on my rail journey!
Sonoma-Marin Area Rail Transit, or SMART for short, is a railway system running in the Sonoma and Marin counties of Northern California. It has a whooping one service, running from Larkspur to Windsor. It is part of the wider Bay Area transit system and as such, can be paid for with the Clipper card. So you can use various forms of public transport from various companies and never know the difference - convenient! The SMART service runs on a single-track rail line that is in total 77 km long. I took the train after reaching Larkspur by bike from San Francisco, tired and eager to return to San Rafael. The SMART trains are of course also equipped with bicycle racks.
So in short, all I saw was:

  • A SMART Nippon Sharyo diesel multiple unit. Surprisingly quiet and smooth for a DMU, with a very nice, comfortable interior.
